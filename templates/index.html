— Aeloria. The Docks. The air smells of salt and iron. —

The lanterns along the Docks sway in the harbour wind, casting long amber shadows across wet cobblestone. Gulls cry somewhere above the mist. A merchant's cart has overturned ahead, spilling grain across the road—three men argue in low, urgent voices nearby.

Your coat is damp. The letter from Lyssa sits heavy against your chest. The Leyline hums faintly beneath your feet.

Warden: I approach the arguing men and listen to what they say.

You drift closer, keeping your hood drawn. One of the men—broad-shouldered, a dockworker by the look of his hands—jabs a finger into the chest of another.

"Three crates gone. Not spilled—gone. Someone moved them before the cart turned."

The third man, quieter, glances at you once. His eyes don't linger.
